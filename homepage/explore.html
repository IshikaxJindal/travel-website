BHANGARH FORT

HISTORY:

Built in the 17th century by Raja Bhagwant Das, the younger brother of Man Singh I, a general of the Mughal Emperor Akbar. Designed as a fortified city with palaces, temples, and a thriving population. The city mysteriously declined and became deserted in the 18th century.

RUMORS:

Many visitors and locals claim to have experienced paranormal activities, including strange noises, unexplained feelings of dread, and sudden electronic equipment failures. The Archaeological Survey of India has placed a sign prohibiting visitors from staying after sunset, citing the paranormal activity as the reason.

Jaswant Thada

Architectural Marvel: The cenotaph is a stunning example of white marble architecture, adorned with intricate carvings and delicate jalis (latticework). Royal Connection: It serves as the cremation ground for the royal family of Marwar, making it a sacred site. Historical Portraits: The walls are adorned with portraits of the Rathore rulers, offering a glimpse into the rich history of the region. Serene Ambiance: The peaceful atmosphere and beautiful surroundings make it a popular spot for meditation and relaxation.
